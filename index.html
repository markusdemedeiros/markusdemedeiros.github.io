About Me

I'm Markus, a PhD student at NYU working with Joseph Tassarotti. I am broadly interested in formal methods, namely in verifying challenging properties of randomized systems at a realistic scale.

Here are some projects I've worked on:

  • Iris-Lean: a port of the Iris logic to the Lean theorem prover.
  • Clutch: a family of program logics for verifying properties of randomized programs in Rocq.
  • SampCert: a differential privacy library which is verified in Lean and deployed at AWS.
  • Prusti: developing an embedding of the Rust type system in a separation logic.
